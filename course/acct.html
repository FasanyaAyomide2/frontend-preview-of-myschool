Course Overview

The Accountancy program at Federal Polytechnic, Ile-Oluji, is designed to equip students with comprehensive knowledge and practical skills in accounting and financial management. The curriculum emphasizes both theoretical understanding and practical application, preparing graduates for various roles in the accounting profession.

Program Structure

The Accountancy program is structured into two main phases:
1. National Diploma (ND): This initial phase introduces students to focus on Foundational accounting principles, business law, and financial management.
2. Higher National Diploma (HND): Building upon the ND foundation, the HND phase offers Advanced accounting topics, auditing, taxation, and financial reporting.

Course Overview:

While specific course titles and descriptions may vary, the Accountancy curriculum typically covers the following core areas:

  • Introduction to Accounting
  • Business Mathematics
  • Business Communication
  • Principles of Economics
  • Business Law

  • Cost Accounting I, II, III

  • Advanced Taxation I, II
  • Public Sector Accounting
  • Auditing & Investigations

  • Audit Practice & Assurance Service
  • Multi-Disciplinary Case Study

FAQ

To apply to the Federal Polytechnic Ile-Oluji, you must first register for the Joint Admissions and Matriculation Board (JAMB) exam and select the institution as your preferred choice. After obtaining a suitable score, visit the polytechnic's official website or admission portal to complete the application form. You will be required to provide your JAMB registration number, personal details, and upload necessary documents such as O'Level results. Once submitted, follow up on admission updates via the school portal or JAMB CAPS to accept your admission offer when given.

The Joint Admissions and Matriculation Board (JAMB) has set a general cut-off mark of 180 for all courses offered by Federal Polytechnic, Ile-Oluji.
However, specific departmental cut-off marks may vary. For the Accountancy program, candidates are advised to aim for a higher score to enhance their admission prospects

To gain admission into the SLT program at FEDPOLEL, candidates must meet the following qualifications:
The entry requirements for the National Diploma (ND) in Accountancy include a minimum of five credit passes in SSCE/NECO/GCE/NABTEB O’ level exams, obtained in not more than two sittings. Mandatory subjects are English Language, Mathematics, and three others from a specified list, such as Economics or Principles of Accounts. Candidates must also meet the required UTME cut-off mark with English Language, Mathematics, and two relevant subjects. For the Higher National Diploma (HND) in Accountancy, applicants need an ND in Accountancy with at least a Lower Credit grade and one year of Industrial Training (IT) or a Pass grade with two years of IT experience. UTME requirements also apply, with similar subject specifications. Both programs are offered at Federal Polytechnic, Ile Oluji.
