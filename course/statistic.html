Course Overview

The Statistics Department at Federal Polytechnic, Ile-Oluji, established in 2016, offers a comprehensive National Diploma (ND) program designed to equip students with both theoretical and practical knowledge in statistical methods and data analysis.

Aim and Objectives

The program is designed to equip students with the skills necessary for:
1. Collecting, organizing, summarizing, presenting, analyzing, and interpreting data for effective management decisions.
2. Ensuring compliance with global best practices in statistical methodologies.

Course Overview:

The ND in Statistics spans two years, divided into four semesters. The curriculum encompasses a range of courses aimed at building a solid foundation in statistics and related fields. While specific course descriptions are not detailed on the official website, the program typically includes subjects such as:

  • Introduction to Statistics
  • Probability Theory
  • Statistical Inference
  • Regression Analysis
  • Statistical Computing
  • Business Statistics

Study Options:

FAQ

To apply to the Federal Polytechnic Ile-Oluji, you must first register for the Joint Admissions and Matriculation Board (JAMB) exam and select the institution as your preferred choice. After obtaining a suitable score, visit the polytechnic's official website or admission portal to complete the application form. You will be required to provide your JAMB registration number, personal details, and upload necessary documents such as O'Level results. Once submitted, follow up on admission updates via the school portal or JAMB CAPS to accept your admission offer when given.

The minimum JAMB cut-off mark for Statistics at FEDPOLEL is generally 150, though this may vary slightly depending on the academic year and the number of applicants. To improve your chances of admission, it is recommended to score well above this benchmark, as Statistics is a competitive course.

To gain admission into the Statistics program at FEDPOLEL, candidates must meet the following qualifications:
- Possess at least five (5) O'Level credits in subjects including English Language, Mathematics, Chemistry, Physics, and Biology. These must be obtained in not more than two sittings in WAEC, NECO, or NABTEB examinations.
- Meet the JAMB cut-off mark and choose Statistics as a course during the JAMB registration.
For Higher National Diploma (HND) applicants, candidates must hold a National Diploma (ND) in Statistics with at least a Lower Credit grade and complete a one-year industrial training program.
